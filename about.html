Meet JoLynne

As an amateur photographer, I continue learning and my work continues improving. New subjects and challenges are the chemistry developing my work. I have long enjoyed the meditative quality of observing the natural world. That said, I am also drawn to photograph the built world through an admiring eye. And more recently I have set myself the challenge of learning more about working with light by photographing both still lifes and portraits in the studio.


To the casual observer, my exploration and selection of such a wide array of subjects might seem indiscriminate, although I prefer to call it eclectic. People familiar with my compositional style often describe it as minimalist, but I know it is not. Perhaps what they are referring to is my use of negative space, which I think allows the subject room to breathe. Also, I favor emotion over technique. If given a choice between a technically perfect photograph with no soul and a slightly out-of-focus image conveying emotion, I'd choose emotion every time.


That said, I do keep developing my technical skills. As a child, my fondness for photography started with the analog: a Polaroid with self-developing film and a Kodak Instamatic with those cube-shaped flashbulbs. In college, one of the requirements for my Bachelor of Science in journalism degree was to take a photojournalism class, where I learned to use a 35mm film camera and to develop my own black-and-white images. Today I use a Nikon D5000, I shoot in color as well as monochrome, and I and am fascinated by the technology of Photoshop.
